Co-op Work Term Report – Fall 2025

Name: Prateek Hakim Khatri
Program: Bachelor of Computing (Co-op)
Work Term: Fall 2025
Role: Data Analyst Co-op Student

Abstract / Introduction

During the Fall 2025 co-op work term, I worked as a Data Analyst Co-op Student supporting data governance, public data access, and analytical initiatives. My role focused on maintaining and improving datasets within the Ontario Data Catalogue, responding to public user inquiries, and supporting validation, documentation, and reporting processes. This report outlines my responsibilities, learning outcomes, key contributions, and reflections from the work term.

Employer Overview

My work term took place in a public-sector data and analytics environment responsible for managing and supporting the Ontario Data Catalogue. This platform provides open access to authoritative datasets used by the public, researchers, and government stakeholders. The organization prioritizes data quality, transparency, and governance to ensure accessible and reliable information.

Job Description

As a Data Analyst Co-op Student, I supported both operational and technical workflows related to public data publishing and governance.

  • Supported scheduled dataset publication and updates in CKAN
  • Reviewed dataset metadata and validation results
  • Responded to user questions and routed requests through Jira
  • Assisted with data standards and artifacts cataloguing
  • Explored reporting and dashboard development using Power BI
  • Configured a local CKAN environment using a Linux virtual machine

Goals and Learning Outcomes

At the start of the work term, my goals included developing a strong understanding of data governance practices, gaining hands-on experience with CKAN and dataset validation, improving reporting skills in Power BI, and strengthening professional communication skills.

Throughout the work term, I successfully achieved most of these goals by participating in dataset maintenance activities, user inquiry workflows, and technical exploration projects. While some reporting initiatives were limited by system constraints, the exploration process provided valuable insight into real-world technical limitations and solution design.

Work Term Experience and Key Contributions

A significant portion of my role involved supporting the Ontario Data Catalogue by validating datasets, identifying quality issues, and coordinating updates. I gained practical experience understanding how datasets are structured, published, and maintained in a production environment.

I also supported responses to public user questions by reviewing incoming requests, determining next steps, and routing issues to appropriate data leads using Jira. This work emphasized the importance of accurate data, clearly written metadata, and timely communication.

Standards and Artifacts Catalogue Project

I contributed to a project focused on consolidating organizational data standards and artifacts into a structured catalogue. My contributions included organizing standards into a consistent format, validating links and references, identifying inconsistencies, and supporting the generation of clear descriptions. This work established a foundation for improved documentation and future dashboard access.

Reporting and Dashboard Development

Using the standards catalogue as a data source, I supported the planning and early development of a Power BI dashboard designed to improve discovery and navigation of data standards. I gained exposure to data modeling, layout standards, and business requirements for internal dashboards.

Leadership and Professional Development

In addition to my technical responsibilities, I served as a United Way Divisional Lead, supporting fundraising initiatives and engagement events. This role strengthened my leadership, coordination, and communication skills. I also completed First Aid certification and participated in professional training related to artificial intelligence.

Lessons Learned

This work term highlighted the importance of data governance, user-focused quality, and cross-functional collaboration. I learned how structured data and metadata impact real users, and how effective communication supports successful data management initiatives.

Conclusion

Overall, the Fall 2025 co-op work term was a valuable learning experience that strengthened my technical, analytical, and professional skills. The experience provided practical insight into public-sector data systems and prepared me well for future co-op terms and a career in data analytics and computing.

Acknowledgements

I would like to thank my supervisors, team members, and data leads for their guidance, feedback, and support throughout the work term.
